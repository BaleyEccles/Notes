* Steady-state tracking of observer-based systems
\[H_{CL}(s) = C(sI - A + Bk)^{-1}B)\]
\[G = -(C(A-Bk)^{-1}B)^{-1}\]

** Servomechanism Design
Assumptions:
1. The open-loop plant model is controllable and observable
2. The open loop plant model has no pole at $s=0$
3. The open loop plant model has no zero at $s=0$

Our obser-based compensator takes the form:
\begin{cases}
\dot{\eta}(t) &= r(t) - y(t) \\
\hat{\dot{x}}(t) &= (A - LC)\hat{x}(t) + Bu(t) + Ly(t) \\
u(t) &= -k\hat{x}(t) + K_e\eta(t)
\end{cases}

\[\begin{cases}
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{\eta}(t) \\
\hat{\dot{x}}(t) \\
\end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 \\
BK_e & A - BK - LC
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
\eta(t) \\
\hat{x}(t) \\
\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}
1 \\ 0
\end{bmatrix}r(t) + \begin{bmatrix}
-1 \\ L
\end{bmatrix}y(t) \\
u(t) &= \begin{bmatrix}
K_e & -K
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
\eta(t) \\ \hat{x}(t)
\end{bmatrix}\end{cases}\]

** Plant + Observer + controller + servomechanism closed-loop:
\[\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{x}(t) \\
\dot{\eta}(t) \\
\hat{\dot{x}}(t) \\
\end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix}
A & BK_e & -BK \\
-C & 0 & 0\\
LC &BK_e & A-BK-LC
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x(t) \\
\eta(t) \\
\hat{x}(t) \\
\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}
0 \\ 1 \\ 0
\end{bmatrix}r(t)\]

\[y(t) = \begin{bmatrix}
C & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x(t) \\ \eta(t) \\ \hat{x}(t)
\end{bmatrix}\]

Using $e_x(t) = x(t) - \hat{x}(t) and $\dot{e_x}(t) = (A - LC)e_x(t)$:
$n+1$ [[id:e7ad3ee3-7394-40ed-b2a3-ca0815bd9280][Eigenvalues]] are determined by : $\begin{bmatrix} A-BK & BK_e \\ -C & 0 \end{bmatrix}$
$n$ [[id:e7ad3ee3-7394-40ed-b2a3-ca0815bd9280][Eigenvalues]] are determined by : $\begin{bmatrix} A-LC \end{bmatrix}$
The new state $\eta(t)$ _does not_ destabilise the system

Let the step be $r(t) = R$, $t\geq 0$
Use the control law in steady-state: $u_{ss} = -K(x_{ss} - e_{x_{ss}}) + K_e\eta_{ss}$
 - $A - LC$ must be non-singular
$e_{x_{ss} = 0}$
Which means:
\[y_{ss} = R\]
 - Perfect tracking
