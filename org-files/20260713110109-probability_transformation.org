* Probability Transformation
If we have distribution for a random variable $X$ and $Y = g(X)$, then we can see that:
\[F_Y(y) = \Pr\{Y \leq y\} = \Pr\{g(X) \leq y\}\]
1. $g$ increasing
   - $F_Y(y) = \Pr\{X \leq g^{-1}(y)\} = F_X(g^{-1}(y)$
2. $g$ decreasing
   - $F_Y(y) = \Pr\{X \geq g^{-1}(y)\} = 1 - F_X(g^{-1}(y))$
Look to the chain rule for $f_Y(y) = \frac{dF_y}{dy}$:
For 1.
\begin{align*}
f_y(y) &= \frac{dF_X(g^{-1}(y)}{dy} \\
&= f_X(g^{-1}(y))\frac{d}{dy}g^{-1}(y)
\end{align*}
For 2.
\begin{align*}
f_y(y) &= -f_X(g^{-1}(y))\frac{d}{dy}g^{-1}(y)
\end{align*}

We combine them:
\[f_Y(y) = f_X(g^{-1}(y))\left|\frac{dx}{dy}\right|\]

\[\gamma = \alpha^2 \frac{E_s}{N_0} = \Omega \frac{E_s}{N_0}\]

\begin{align*}
\gamma &= g(\alpha) = \alpha^2 \frac{E_s}{N_0} \\
\Rightarrow \alpha &= g^{-1}(\gamma) = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{E_s/N_0}} \\
\left|\frac{d\alpha}{d\gamma}\right| &= \frac{1}{2\frac{E_s}{N_0}\sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{E_s/N_0}}} \\
f_{\gamma}(\gamma) &= f_{\alpha}(g^{-1}(\gamma))\left|\frac{d\alpha}{d\gamma}\right| \\
&= \frac{f_{\alpha}(\sqrt{\frac{\Omega\gamma}{\bar{\gamma}}})}{2\sqrt{\frac{\gamma\bar{\gamma}}{\Omega}}}
\end{align*}
