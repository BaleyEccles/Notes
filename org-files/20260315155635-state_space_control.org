** Advantages
 - Non-linear systems
 - MIMO  (Not just SISO)
   - Multiple Inputs Multiple Outputs
 - State describes distribution of energy in a systems
 - Direct placement of closed-loop poles
   - Although, we cannot place closed loop zeros
 - Optimal control

** ODE (Linear state equations)
 - \[\dot{\underline{x}} = f(\underline{x},t)\]
 - \[\dot{\underline{x}} = A\underline{x} + B \underline{u}\]
 - \[\underline{y} = C\underline{x} + D \underline{u}\]
   
** LTI
\[\sum_{j=0}^na_{n-j}\frac{d^jy}{dt^j} = \sum_{k=0}^nb_{n-k} \frac{d^ku}{dt^k}\]
where $a_0 = 1$ and all $a_j$, $b_k$ are constants

*** Choose states
\[x_n = \frac{d^ny}{dt^n} - \beta_0 \frac{d^{n-1}u}{dt^{n-1}} - \beta_1 \frac{d^{n-2}u}{dt^{n-2}} - \hdots - \beta_{n-2} \frac{du}{dt} - \beta_{n-1}u = \frac{dx_{n-1}}{dt} - \beta_{n-1}u\]
\[\beta_{n-1} = \beta_{n-1} - a_1\beta_{n-2} - a_2 \beta_{n-3}  - \hdots - a_{n-2}\beta_1 a_{n-1}\beta_0\]

This guarantees the existence and uniqueness of the solution of the equations
We obtain:
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{x}_{1}\\
\dot{x}_{2}\\
\vdots \\
\dot{x}_{n-1}\\
\dot{x}_{n}
\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1 & 0 & \hdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & \hdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \hdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & \hdots & 1 \\
-a_n & -a_{n-1} & -a_{n-2} & \hdots & -a_1
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x_{1}\\
x_{2}\\
\vdots \\
x_{n-1} \\
x_{n}
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
\beta_{1}\\
\beta_{2}\\
\vdots \\
\beta_{n-1} \\
\beta_{n}
\end{bmatrix}
\[y = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 & \hdots & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x_{1}\\
x_{2}\\
\vdots \\
x_{n-1} \\
x_{n}
\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}\beta_0\end{bmatrix}u\]

**** Special case
If we only have $\sum_{j=0}^na_{n-j}\frac{d^jy}{dt^j}d = b_nu$:
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{x}_{1}\\
\dot{x}_{2}\\
\vdots \\
\dot{x}_{n-1}\\
\dot{x}_{n}
\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1 & 0 & \hdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & \hdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \hdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & \hdots & 1 \\
-a_n & -a_{n-1} & -a_{n-2} & \hdots & -a_1
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x_{1}\\
x_{2}\\
\vdots \\
x_{n-1} \\
x_{n}
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
0 \\
\vdots \\
0 \\
\beta_{n}
\end{bmatrix}
\[y = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 & \hdots & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
x_{1}\\
x_{2}\\
\vdots \\
x_{n-1} \\
x_{n}
\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}0\end{bmatrix}u\]
