* Fading
 -  
\[Tx\{s(t)\} \rightarrow |h| \rightarrow |h+n(t)| \rightarrow Rx\]
 - $h$ can vary over time and can be multiple samples long
\[s(t) = \Re\{s_0(t)e^{j2\pi f_ct}\} = s_{\alpha}(t)\cos(2\pi f_ct) - s_{\alpha}(t)\sin(2\pi f_ct)\]

\[r(t) &= \Re\left\{\sum_{k=1}^N a_k s_0(t - T_k) e^{j2\pi f_c(t - T_k) + j\phi_k}\right\} + n(t)\]
 - The noise ($n(t)$) is typically small
This has [[id:67259b4a-7fd8-4d7b-afaf-42c443f305a6][baseband]] equivalent:
\[r_0(t) = \sum_{k=1}^N a_k s_0(t - T_k) e^{j(\phi_c - 2\pi f_cT_k)} + n(t)\]
This meas there are a channel impulse response:
\[h(t,\tau) = \sum_{k=1}^N a_k \delta (t - T_k) e^{j(\phi_k - 2\pi f_c \tau_k)}\]

** Amount of Fading
Additionally, define the Amount of Fading (AF):
\[AF = \frac{Var[\alpha^2]}{(E[\alpha^2])^2} = \frac{E[\gamma^2] - (E[\gamma])^2}{(E[\gamma])^2}\]
 - Tends to be independent of $\Omega$

** Fast & Slow Fading
[[id:b46c0234-ae60-47ca-b311-c89820a62fd4][Coherence Time]]: $\Delta \tau \propto \frac{1}{f_{d}}$
 - $f_d$ is the [[id:02463fc8-1fc6-427f-acd1-03542a688e16][Doppler Shift Frequency]]
\[\Delta \tau = \frac{1}{f_{d_{\max}}}\sqrt{\frac{9}{16\pi}}\]
If $T_s < \Delta \tau$, we have slow fading
If $T_s > \Delta \tau$, we have fast fading

** Flat & Frequency-Selective Fading
If the frequency profile is affected uniformly, we have Flat Fading
If the frequency profile is affected non-uniformly, we have Frequency-Selective Fading

A flat response occurs when the transmitted bandwidth is smaller than the coherence bandwidth ($\Delta f$)
\[\Delta f \propto \frac{1}{\tau_{\max}}\]
A frequency-selective response occurs when the transmitted bandwidth is greater than the coherence bandwidth

** Narrowband Fading Model
 - All paths arrive at the same time (assumption)
\[r(t) = \Re\left\{e^{j2\pi f_c t} s_0(t) \sum_{k=1}^N a_k e^{j\phi_k}\right\} + n(t)\]
\[r(t) = \Re\left\{e^{j2\pi f_c t} s_0(t) \alpha(t) + n(t) \right\}\]

Equivalent baseband:
[[xopp-figure:/home/baley/UTAS/org-roam/org-files/Narrowband_Fading_Model_Baseband.xopp]]
The statistics of $\alpha(t)$ are affected by all scales
 - Mean power level of $E\{\alpha^2(t)\} = \Omega$
   1. Including large and medium loss
      - $\Omega = P_t K\left(\frac{d_0}{d}\right)^n \psi$
        - $\psi \sim N\{0, \sigma_s^2\}$
        - $P_t K\left(\frac{d_0}{d}\right)^n$ Large-scale (environmental model)
   2. Excluding large and medium loss
      - $\Omega = 1$
