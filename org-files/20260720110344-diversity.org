Spatial, time, frequency and multi-path variants.
** Spatial Diversity
One transmitter ($Tx$) to multiple receivers ($Rx_1, Rx_2, Rx_3, \hdots, Rx_N$). Each ave their own fading.($\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \hdots, \alpha_N$).
 - Assume all paths are independent and identically distributed (iid).
 - For examples assume Rayleigh [[id:8c79d3ee-766a-4f72-a070-7a44d0f85ac1][Fading]] ($f_{\gamma}(\gamma) = \frac{1}{\bar{\gamma}}e^{-\frac{\gamma}{\bar{\gamma}}}\ \bar{\gamma} \geq 0$)

\[x = \begin{bmatrix}
x_1 \\
x_2 \\
\vdots \\
x_N \\
\end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}
\alpha_1 \\
\alpha_2 \\
\vdots \\
\alpha_N \\
\end{bmatrix}r + \begin{bmatrix}
n_1 \\
n_2 \\
\vdots \\
n_N \\
\end{bmatrix} = \alpha r + n\]
The output
\begin{align*}
y &= \sum_{k=1}^Nw_k^{*}x_k = w^Hx \\
  &= w^H\alpha r + w^Hn
\end{align*}

Three options for selecting $w$ are:
1. Selection Combining (SC)
   - Simple 
2. Maximum Ratio Combining (MRC)
   - Most complex
3. Equal Gain Combining (EGC)
   - Between complexity

*** Selection Combining (SC)
The antenna with the highest [[id:13d613eb-9630-41af-ab3f-c15eabc686f5][SNR]] is selected in preference to all the others.
\[w_i = \begin{cases}
1 &, \gamma = \max_k\{\gamma_k\} \\
0 &, \textrm{otherwise}
\end{cases}\]

\[\therefore \textrm{Output SNR} = \gamma_{out} = \max_k\{\gamma_k\}\]

**** Outage probability:
\begin{align*}
P_{out} &= \Pr[\gamma_{out} < \gamma_{th}] \\
&= \Pr\left[\gamma_1, \gamma_2, \gamma_3, \hdots, \gamma_N < \gamma_{th}\right] \\
&= \left(1-e^{-\gamma_{th}/\bar{\gamma}}\right)^N
\end{align*}

As $\bar{\gamma} \rightarrow \infty$:
\[P_{out} &= \left(\frac{\gamma_{th}}{\bar{\gamma}}\right)^N \propto \frac{1}{\bar{\gamma}^N}\]

f_{\gamma_{SC}}(\gamma) &= \frac{N}{\bar{\gamma}}e^{-\gamma/\bar{\gamma}}(1 - e^{\gamma/\bar{\gamma}})^{N-1}

*** Maximum Ratio Combining (MRC)
\begin{align*}
y &= w^H\alpha r + w^Hn \\
\gamma_{out} &= \frac{E\{|w^H\alpha r|^2\}}{E\{|w^Hn|^2\}} \\
&\textrm{A good channel estimate means $\alpha$ is known} \\
\gamma_{out} &= \frac{|w^H\alpha|^2E\{|r|^2\}}{|w^H|^2E\{n^2\}} \\
\gamma_{out} &= \frac{|w^H\alpha|^2P_r}{|w^H|^2\sigma^2} \\
\end{align*}
So, choose $w$ such that:
\[w_{MRC} = \max_w\{\gamma_{out}\} = \max_w[\frac{|w^H\alpha|^2}{\sigma^2|w|^2}]\]
By Cauchy-Schwarz, $w=\alpha$
\[y = r\sum_{k=1}^N|\alpha_k|^2 + n\]
and
\[\gamma_{out} = \sum_{k=1}^N\gamma_k\]

\[M_{\gamma_{out}}(s) = \frac{1}{(1 - s\bar{\gamma})^N}\]
\[f_{\gamma_{out}} = \frac{1}{(N-1)!}\frac{\gamma_{out}^{N-1}}{\bar{\gamma}^N}e^{\gamma_{out}/\bar{\gamma}}\]

**** Average SNR
\[\gamma_{out} = \sum_{k=1}^N\gamma_k\]
\[E\{\gamma_{out}\} = N\bar{\gamma}\]
**** Outage Probability
\[P_{out} = 1 - e^{-\gamma_{th}/\bar{\gamma}}\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}(\frac{\gamma_{th}}{\bar{\gamma}})^k\frac{1}{k!}\]
As $\bar{\gamma} \rightarrow \infty$, $P_{out}\propto \frac{1}{\bar{\gamma}^N}$
