* Optimal Control Theory
Consider the general [[id:91abfc71-ac31-4490-8d23-2d216b8fe303][state space]] equation:
\[\dot{x}(t) = g(x(t), u(t), t),\ x(t_0) = x_0\]
We introduce a performance index/cost function/objective function. This is to be minimised:
\[J = \int_{t_0}^{t_f}L(x(t), u(t), t)\ dt + \phi[x(t_f), t_f]\]
 - $L(x(t), u(t), t)$ is the loss function
   - It assigns penalty to $x$ and $u$
 - $\phi[x(t_f), t_f]$ is the terminal cost
   - Can penalise the state $x$ based on the time it achieves the final result
The goal is to get $\min\{J\}$ from some $x^{*}(t)$ and $u^{ *}(t)$

** Quadratic Performance Indices
\begin{align*}
J &= \int_{t_0}^{t_f}\left[\left(q_{11}x_1^2 + q_{22}x_2^2 + q_{33}x_3^2 + \hdots\right) + r_1u^2\right]\ dt \\
J &= \int^{t_f}_{t_0}\left(\left[x_1\ x_2\ x_3\ \hdots\right]\begin{bmatrix}
q_{11} & 0 & 0 & \hdots \\
0 & q_{22} & 0 & \hdots \\
0 & 0 & q_{33} & \hdots \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
x_1\\
x_2\\
x_3\\
\vdots
 \end{bmatrix} + [u][r_1][u]\right)\ dt \\
J &= \int_{t_0}^{t_f}(x^TQx + u^TRu) \ dt
\end{align*}
 - $Q$ is the state weighting matrix
 - $R$ is the input weighting matrix
