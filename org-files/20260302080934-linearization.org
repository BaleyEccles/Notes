* Linearization
\[\underline{\dot{x}}(t) = f(\underline{x}(t), \underline{u}(t)) \rightarrow \underline{\dot{x}}\(t) = \underline{A}\underline{x}(t) + \underline{B}\underline{u}(t)\]

** Working
Using [[id:356b1296-2188-4d04-9ccc-a4381bcc02b6][Taylor Series]] around $x = \bar{x}$:
\[f(x) = f(\bar{x}) + f'(\bar{x})(x - \bar{x}) + \hdots\]
There is an equilibrium point at $\dot{x} = f(x) = 0$
\[f(x) = 0 + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}|_{x=\bar{x}}(x - \bar{x}) + \textrm{H.O.T}\]
Choose to transform the state variable:
\begin{align*}
\Delta x &= x - \bar{x} \\
\Rightarrow \Delta \dot{x} &= \dot{x} \\
\Rightarrow \Delta \dot{x} &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}|_{x=\bar{x}}\cdot \Delta x \\
\Delta \dot{\underline{x}} &= \underline{A} \cdot \Delta\underline{x}
\end{align*}

** Non-linear Effects:
 - Finite Escape Time
 - Multiple Isolated Equilibrium points
 - Limit Cycle
    - Fixed f & A oscillation that occur irrespective if initial conditions
